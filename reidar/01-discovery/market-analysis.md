## Executive Summary

The VC intelligence software market sits at approximately $930M–$1B TAM (narrowly defined, 2025) within a broader Investment Management Software category reaching $4.7B–$12.4B, growing at 9–11% CAGR. Incumbents — Affinity, PitchBook, 4Degrees — have consolidated the relationship-layer and data-layer but leave a clear gap in judgment-amplification: tools that learn an individual GP's thesis and surface companies through their specific lens rather than a shared data commodity. Reidar's timing is structurally favorable: 85% of dealmakers now use AI daily (Tier 1 survey data, 2025), sector specialization among funds is accelerating sharply, and the arrival of Rowspace ($50M, Sequoia/Emergence, Feb 2026) validates enterprise appetite for VC AI while leaving the emerging-fund segment explicitly unserved. The central risk is TAM size: the total addressable emerging-fund segment is approximately $50M SAM, requiring capital-efficient GTM and early ARR to prove venture-fundable growth before incumbents ship competing features — a window estimated at 18–24 months.

## Growth Trajectory

### Key Growth Drivers

- **AI adoption rate**: 85% of dealmakers use AI for daily tasks (up from 76% YoY); 94% plan to deepen integration (Tier 1 industry survey, 2025). Demand pull is not speculative.
- **Sector specialization accelerating**: Generalist funds dropped from 22% to 5% of new fund formations between 2020 and 2026. This trend directly expands the market for thesis-specific intelligence tools — a generalist CRM cannot serve a fund specialized in climate biotech.
- **New entrant GPs**: 40% of new emerging managers have no prior VC experience (up from 31% in 2022). These GPs lack institutional sourcing infrastructure and are disproportionately tool-receptive.
- **Agentic AI inflection**: Gartner projects 33% of enterprise software will be agentic by 2028 (less than 1% today). Bessemer data: 234 analyst hours saved via agentic tools. BlackRock: 5x research throughput. These are the proof points Reidar's pitch needs.
- **Harmonic commoditization**: Harmonic's $1.45B valuation signals that shared data platforms have peaked — the marginal value of another data aggregator is declining. This creates the opening for a judgment-layer product that uses data as input, not product.
- **HBR validation** (Tier 1, Nov 2025): "The next generation of VC AI tools will amplify individual investor judgment — what do I know that the market doesn't." This is a direct frame for Reidar's positioning.

### Key Headwinds

- **Small SAM ceiling**: $50M SAM is a hard constraint. Venture economics typically require a path to $1B+ TAM; Reidar must articulate a credible expansion thesis (mid-market funds, international, adjacent workflows).
- **Affinity entrenchment**: Affinity is the default relationship OS for most US VC firms with meaningful AUM. Displacing it requires a workflow reason to switch, not just a better AI feature.
- **Rowspace entering market**: $50M from Sequoia and Emergence (Feb 2026) explicitly targets PE/VC AI data workflows, though current positioning is $100B+ AUM only. Their downstream move to mid-market is a 24–36 month risk, not a day-one threat.
- **Capital concentration**: The top 10 VC firms control a disproportionate share of AUM and budgets. Emerging fund budgets are constrained — the typical stack is $18K–$32K/year total, meaning Reidar must displace existing spend, not expand it.
- **Commoditization of AI features**: Every major CRM and data platform will ship AI features. The differentiation window is real but time-limited.

## Market Maturity Assessment

VC intelligence software is in early-growth phase on the S-curve — past the innovator trough (Affinity and PitchBook proved enterprise willingness to pay), but not yet in the late-growth consolidation that would foreclose new entrants. The specific sub-segment Reidar targets — agentic, thesis-aware deal sourcing for emerging managers — is pre-product-market-fit as a category. The analog here is Salesforce in 2003 versus the broader CRM market: a defined problem, early customer awareness, but no dominant solution in the specific niche. Rowspace's raise is the category-validation signal analogous to a Series B from an adjacent player; it confirms institutional belief in the macro thesis while leaving the emerging-fund segment explicitly uncontested. The window before consolidation is 18–24 months, after which Affinity, HubSpot, or a Rowspace downstream product could plausibly ship a competing feature set.

## Regulatory Summary

**At launch (Year 1 — US only):**
- Reidar is almost certainly a software vendor, not an investment adviser. No SEC registration required, provided marketing materials avoid "recommendation" language and Reidar does not hold or manage capital.
- CCPA Automated Decision-Making Technology (ADMT) rules (effective January 2026) apply to algorithm-based scoring of individuals. Reidar's scoring of founders and companies falls within scope — opt-out mechanisms and audit trails are required from day one. This is a compliance build, not a blocker.
- SEC Predictive Data Analytics AI rule withdrawn (June 2025) — net positive; removes a potential compliance burden that was never fully enacted.

**Key compliance milestones and costs:**
| Milestone | Timing | Cost Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| CCPA ADMT opt-out + audit trail | Launch | Engineering cost only (~$10K–$20K) |
| SOC 2 Type 1 | Year 1 | $25K–$65K |
| SOC 2 Type 2 + pen test | Year 2 | $50K–$100K (incremental) |
| EU AI Act compliance workstream | 6–12 months before EU launch (est. 2028) | $50K–$100K+ |
| Cumulative US compliance (Years 1–3) | — | $100K–$220K total |

**EU expansion:** The EU AI Act classifies investment optimization AI as HIGH RISK. Do not enter EU markets without a dedicated compliance workstream and outside counsel. Earliest viable EU entry: 2028. UK may be viable earlier given lighter AI regulation post-Brexit.

**Overall regulatory risk at US launch: LOW TO MEDIUM.**

## Geographic Analysis

**Beachhead: NYC Emerging Managers (Fund I–III, $10M–$150M AUM)**

NYC is the correct beachhead market over SF Bay Area despite SF having 60–65% of major VC firms. The reasoning:

1. **Density with accessibility**: NYC has 100+ active VC firms and is the fastest-growing non-SV hub, with cold outreach culture meaningfully more open than SF relationship-gatekeeping norms.
2. **Structural tailwind**: New York State launched a $102M emerging manager program in 2025, directly seeding the exact fund cohort Reidar targets and creating concentrated demand.
3. **Differentiation hunger**: NYC emerging managers compete against established SF networks without equivalent information advantages — they are more motivated to adopt tools that systematize sourcing.
4. **Beachhead win condition**: 10 paying NYC customers within 6 months of launch; 3 referenceable for case studies.

**Expansion path:**

| Phase | Markets | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Beachhead | NYC emerging managers | Year 1 |
| Tier 2 expansion | Boston, Miami, LA | Year 2 |
| Tier 3 expansion | Austin, Denver/Boulder, Chicago | Year 3 |
| International | UK (lighter AI regulation) | Year 4 |
| EU | EU (post-AI Act compliance) | 2028+ |

SF Bay Area is a later market, not an early one — the concentration of sophisticated buyers creates tougher procurement processes and stronger incumbent relationships. Enter SF after product-market fit is established and references exist.

## Timing Assessment: Why Now?

**Structural tailwinds converging in 2026:**

1. AI adoption has crossed the workflow threshold (85% daily use) — the customer education problem is largely solved. Pitching AI-assisted sourcing no longer requires convincing GPs that AI is real.
2. Harmonic's peak valuation signals shared data commoditization — the marginal basis point of better data is declining in value. This creates the opening for a judgment layer above the data layer.
3. Sector specialization is accelerating — 95% of new funds have a defined thesis (vs. 78% in 2020), expanding the addressable market for thesis-specific tools.
4. 40% of new GPs have no prior institutional sourcing infrastructure — the highest proportion ever recorded, and these GPs are the most tool-receptive cohort.

**The 18–24 month window:**

Affinity and Harmonic could plausibly ship judgment-learning features — the technical capability is available to any well-capitalized team. The window closes when one of the following occurs: (a) Affinity ships an agentic thesis-learning layer with meaningful beta adoption, (b) Rowspace expands downstream to sub-$150M AUM funds, or (c) a well-funded vertical entrant raises $10M+ specifically for the emerging-manager segment. None of these has happened as of March 2026.

**What would close the window:**
- Affinity Series D announcement with AI-native sourcing as a headline feature
- Rowspace announcing a "Startup" or "Emerging Manager" tier
- A direct competitor raising $5M+ in the same segment with press coverage
- A platform player (Salesforce Ventures, HubSpot) acquiring a vertical VC CRM

## Data Gaps

The following gaps should be addressed in subsequent research phases before financial modeling (Phase 7) and investor materials (Phase 9):

1. **Willingness-to-pay validation**: ACV benchmarks are derived from public pricing and analyst estimates, not primary customer interviews. The $15K–$25K ACV assumption requires validation with 5–10 target customers before committing to a pricing model.

2. **Fund lifecycle churn quantification**: Structural churn from fund wind-downs is acknowledged but not quantified. Need primary data on average fund tenure, GP re-fund rate, and Fund II upgrade conversion before building an accurate LTV model.

3. **Affinity AI roadmap**: Affinity's product direction is the single highest-stakes competitive unknown. No public roadmap data was available for this analysis. Requires monitoring product releases, job postings (ML engineer hires), and beta user feedback.

4. **NYC emerging manager count (primary)**: The ~2,500 US emerging fund estimate is NVCA-derived. A tighter NYC-specific count (likely 200–400 active funds) is needed for beachhead TAM and sales capacity modeling.

5. **Rowspace downstream timeline**: Rowspace's current positioning is $100B+ AUM only, but their expansion timeline to mid-market is unknown. This is a category-defining risk variable; monitoring their hiring (mid-market AE roles, SMB product roles) will provide early signal.

6. **CAC empirical baseline**: CAC estimates ($5K–$15K) are category analogs, not primary data. The actual CAC for founder-led sales into NYC emerging managers may be significantly lower (warm network) or higher (longer sales cycle than expected). First 10 customers will establish the real baseline.

7. **Regulatory interpretation of CCPA ADMT scope**: Whether Reidar's company scoring (not individual scoring) falls within CCPA ADMT scope is an open legal question. A brief outside counsel opinion ($2K–$5K) should resolve this before launch.
