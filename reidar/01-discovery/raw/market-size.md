## SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)

### Target Segment: US-based VC firms, emerging/small funds (<$150M AUM, 1–5 GPs)

**Step 1: How many VC firms exist in the US?**

- NVCA 2025 Yearbook (Tier 1): 538 funds raised capital in 2024; 3,417 total VC firms as of end-2023
- PitchBook data: ~6,175 active VC firms (down from 8,315 peak in 2021)
- Median US fund size: $21.3M (NVCA 2025 Yearbook)
- Median fund size outside CA/NY/MA hubs: $10M

The wide range (538 actively fundraising vs. 6,175 "active") reflects different definitions. The most defensible figure for "operating, deploying capital" is approximately 3,000–4,000 US VC firms.

**Step 2: How many are emerging/small (<$150M AUM)?**

- In 2024, emerging managers raised 20% of total capital ($15B) across 245 funds (Juniper Square / NVCA data)
- Median fund size is $21.3M, meaning the majority of funds by count are small
- Industry estimates: ~70–75% of all VC funds are sub-$150M by AUM (consistent with median fund size data)
- Applied to ~3,500 active US firms: approximately 2,400–2,600 qualifying target firms

**Step 3: SAM calculation**

Assuming:
- 2,500 target US VC firms (emerging/small, <$150M AUM)
- Average annual software spend per firm on intelligence/CRM/sourcing tools: $15,000–$25,000 (derived from pricing benchmarks below; conservative for small funds)

**SAM = 2,500 firms × $15,000–$25,000 ACV = $37.5M–$62.5M**

Mid-point: ~$50M SAM for US emerging VC firms specifically.

If the beachhead expands to all US VC firms (3,500 firms) at similar spend:
**Expanded US SAM = 3,500 × $20,000 = $70M**

North America is 40% of the global VC software market, so global SAM scales to ~$175M at equivalent penetration.

## SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

### Year 1 (2026–2027): Early Adopter Phase

Assumptions:
- Founder-led sales, product-led growth / referral within VC networks
- Target: 25–50 paying customers at $12,000–$18,000 ACV (emerging fund tier)
- Win rate on qualified pipeline: ~20–30% (typical early B2B SaaS)
- Sales cycle: 60–120 days for emerging managers (shorter than enterprise)

**Year 1 SOM: 30–50 customers × $15,000 ACV = $450K–$750K ARR**

### Year 2: Network Effect + Category Awareness

Assumptions:
- Referrals from GP networks accelerate pipeline
- Expand to 100–200 customers
- ACV creeps up as larger funds adopt ($20K–$30K range)

**Year 2 SOM: 150 customers × $20,000 ACV = $3M ARR**

### Year 3: Market Penetration

Assumptions:
- 1–2% penetration of 2,500 target firms = 25–50 initially, scaling to 3–5% = 75–125 firms
- Upmarket expansion to mid-tier funds ($150M–$500M AUM) begins
- ACV blended: $25,000–$35,000

**Year 3 SOM: 300–400 customers × $25,000 ACV = $7.5M–$10M ARR**

These figures are conservative. Comparable VC tooling companies (Affinity raised $80M+ at significant ARR; 4Degrees reached mid-market scale within 3 years) suggest the ceiling is much higher for a product that owns the judgment layer rather than just the CRM layer.

## Unit Economics Benchmarks

### What VCs Pay for Tools Today

**Affinity CRM (market leader for VC CRM)**
- Essential: $2,000/user/year
- Scale: $2,300/user/year
- Advanced (most popular): $2,700/user/year (includes AI meeting intelligence)
- Enterprise: custom pricing
- A 3-person GP team on Advanced = $8,100/year just for the CRM

**4Degrees (Affinity alternative)**
- Approximately $100/user/month = $1,200/user/year
- Custom quotes required; no published minimums
- Positioned as mid-market alternative to Affinity

**PitchBook (data/intelligence layer)**
- "Solopreneur" plan: $20,000/year (1 seat)
- Average revenue per account (ARPA): $58,300
- Average revenue per user (ARPU): $4,900
- Most emerging funds cannot afford full PitchBook access

**Crunchbase**
- Transparent tiered pricing, significantly cheaper than PitchBook
- Emerging managers default to Crunchbase for data layer

**Visible.vc (LP reporting / portfolio monitoring)**
- Founder-side plans: $0–$249/month
- Investor/VC-side pricing: custom (not published)

**Harmonic.ai (AI deal sourcing)**
- Custom pricing (contact sales); positioned as premium sourcing layer

**Typical emerging fund annual software stack cost (estimate)**

| Tool Category | Representative Tool | Annual Cost (small fund) |
|---|---|---|
| CRM / Relationship Intelligence | Affinity (3 users, Essential) | $6,000 |
| Data / Market Intelligence | Crunchbase Pro | $2,400 |
| Portfolio Monitoring / LP Reporting | Visible.vc | $3,600–$6,000 |
| Deal Sourcing / Startup Database | Harmonic or manual | $3,000–$10,000 |
| Fund Admin / Cap Table | Carta | $3,000–$8,000 |
| **Total** | | **$18,000–$32,000/year** |

This validates the $15,000–$25,000 ACV assumption used in the SAM calculation. An AI investment associate that consolidates multiple layers (CRM + sourcing + memo writing) could be priced at $18,000–$36,000/year for a small fund and still represent a cost-neutral or savings-positive switch.

### CAC Estimates

- B2B SaaS average CAC: $300–$5,000 for SMB/mid-market; higher for enterprise
- Average B2B SaaS sales cycle (2024): 134 days (up from 107 days in early 2022)
- VC-to-VC sales (selling to VCs) benefits from tight network effects — a warm intro from a GP cuts sales cycle significantly
- Estimated CAC for VC tooling at early stage (founder-led, network-driven): $2,000–$5,000 per customer
- CAC payback at $15,000 ACV and ~35% gross margin: ~10–12 months (healthy for SaaS)

### Churn Benchmarks

- Healthy SaaS monthly churn: <1–2% (12–24% annualized)
- VC-specific tooling has structural advantages for retention:
  - Fund lifecycle lock-in: once a fund's deal history, relationships, and memos are in a tool, switching is extremely high-friction
  - GP continuity: same GPs persist across fund cycles; no turnover-driven churn
  - Data network effect: system becomes more valuable over time as it accumulates judgment patterns
- Expected annual churn for a well-adopted VC intelligence product: 5–15%
- Key churn risk: fund dissolution (GPs not raising Fund II) — 2024 data shows many emerging managers from 2020–2022 are struggling to raise successor funds

## Market Headwinds & Risks

### 1. Fragmented, Low-Trust Buyer
VCs are famously skeptical buyers of software. GPs are busy, operationally lean (median fund has 2–3 investment professionals), and accustomed to manual workflows and spreadsheets. Adoption requires behavioral change at the highest-status person in the firm.
- Source: NVCA Yearbook 2025, Affinity 2025 Benchmark Report

### 2. Small TAM Concentration Problem
The total VC software market is ~$930M–$1B in 2025. Even at 10% market share, ARR would be ~$93M — significant but not massive. The path to scale requires expanding beyond emerging VC into larger funds, PE, corporate VC, or fund-of-funds.
- Source: Market.us, Peony market analysis

### 3. Capital Concentration Squeezing Emerging Managers
2024 data shows emerging managers captured only 20% of fundraising capital across 245 funds — and many 2020–2022 vintage emerging managers are failing to raise Fund II. If the beachhead segment shrinks, TAM assumptions need revision.
- Source: Juniper Square / NVCA 2025 Yearbook

### 4. Affinity's Entrenched Position
Affinity has 3,000+ VC/PE customers, has raised $80M+, and is investing heavily in AI (launched AI meeting intelligence in 2025, appointed CTO in early 2025). It is the default CRM for serious VC firms and will not cede ground without a clear product differentiation story.
- Source: Affinity.co pricing page, CRMs Reviewed (Oct 2025)

### 5. Big Players Moving In
Rowspace raised $50M (Sequoia-led, Feb 2026) explicitly for AI-native data workflows for PE/VC. Microsoft Copilot, Salesforce AI, and other horizontal AI platforms are adding workflow automation that could commoditize the memo-writing and summarization layer. Differentiation must go deeper than features.
- Source: Web search results, March 2026

### 6. "Hard to Sell" Dynamics
- Sales cycles are 90–180 days for B2B SaaS broadly; VC buyer complexity is higher
- Partnership decisions at small funds often require GP consensus (no separate IT/procurement function)
- Price sensitivity: emerging managers are cost-conscious; $20K+ ACV is a real hurdle for sub-$30M funds
- Source: B2B SaaS CAC benchmarks, 4Degrees/Affinity positioning materials

### 7. AI Commoditization Risk
The core value prop (AI writes memos, sources deals) is becoming tablestakes as LLM capabilities improve. The durable moat must be the personalized judgment layer — not the AI itself. Without that differentiation being real and demonstrable early, commoditization risk is high.

## Data Gaps

### What Could Not Be Found

1. **Precise count of US VC firms by fund size bucket** (e.g., how many are $10M–$50M vs. $50M–$150M). NVCA Yearbook provides median but not distribution histograms. Gap: contact NVCA directly or purchase PitchBook fund data.

2. **Actual software spend per VC firm, by fund size.** No public survey data found on what an average $30M fund spends annually on software. The $18K–$32K estimate is bottom-up from pricing pages, not from demand-side survey data. Gap: primary research (10–20 GP interviews) would close this.

3. **Affinity's actual ARR and customer count.** Affinity claims "3,000+ customers" but does not disclose revenue. Comparable private company. Gap: Crunchbase/Tracxn may have fundraising-implied estimates; secondary market data sources could help.

4. **Churn rates for VC-specific SaaS.** No published churn benchmarks for the VC tooling category specifically. General B2B SaaS benchmarks used as proxy. Gap: primary research or CB Insights / Meritech SaaS index data.

5. **Willingness-to-pay data for AI investment associate category.** This is a genuinely new product category; pricing for "passive judgment learning" products does not exist in the market yet. Gap: customer discovery interviews with 20+ GPs; conjoint pricing research.

6. **Number of emerging managers likely to raise Fund II in 2026–2028.** This directly affects churn assumptions for the target segment. Gap: NVCA / PitchBook emerging manager cohort data (may require paid access).

### Suggested Primary Research to Fill Gaps
- 15–20 GP interviews at sub-$150M funds: software spend, tool adoption, pain points, WTP
- Survey via VC network (e.g., Emerging Venture Capitalists Association, EVCA) on tech stack composition
- Competitive pricing audit: direct sales conversations with Affinity, Harmonic, 4Degrees to understand packaging/minimums for small funds
