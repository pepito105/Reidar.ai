# Industry Trends: VC Intelligence Software

**Research date:** 2026-03-29
**Searches conducted:** 10 web searches across 4 rounds
**Context:** Market entry analysis for Reidar — an AI investment associate that learns individual investor judgment via passive workflow capture

---

## Technology Trends

### Trend 1: AI Dominance in VC Operations — "The Infrastructure Is Laid"

**Description:** AI has moved from experimental feature to core infrastructure across the VC toolstack. Adoption of AI features in VC platforms nearly quadrupled between Q4 2023 and Q2 2025. 85% of private capital dealmakers now use AI to automate daily tasks (up from 76% a year prior). 95% of PE/VC firms use AI in some capacity for investment decisions. 82% use AI specifically for deal sourcing research.

**Current adoption stage:** Growing → approaching Mature (for tier-1 firms), still Early for emerging funds

**Impact on Reidar:** Opportunity. The infrastructure moment validates the category. Firms have already accepted AI in their workflow — Reidar is not selling a concept, it's selling a differentiated layer. The commoditization of AI-augmented sourcing (Harmonic, Affinity, Crunchbase AI) creates the exact opening Reidar's thesis depends on: everyone has the same data, the edge reverts to judgment.

**Timeline to mainstream:** Already mainstream for top-quartile firms. Emerging fund adoption at ~50%+ by end of 2026.

**Source quality:** Tier 1 — Affinity internal survey (n=300 dealmakers); Tier 2 — Decile Group platform data (581 users of AI features Q2 2025 vs. 153 in Q4 2023)

---

### Trend 2: Passive Data Capture as Foundational CRM Pattern

**Description:** Affinity ($120M raised, 3,000+ customers, 18 trillion emails/calendar events processed) has established passive capture from Gmail/Outlook/Calendar as the expected baseline for VC CRMs. "VC teams save 200+ hours of manual data entry per year" by connecting email providers. The category has moved from "nice to have" to table stakes. 4Degrees, Intapp DealCloud, and Rings.ai all offer similar passive capture layers.

**Current adoption stage:** Growing (among mid/large firms), Early (for emerging funds)

**Impact on Reidar:** Both opportunity and threat. Opportunity: passive capture is validated behavior — GPs already expect their tools to learn from their activity. Reidar can inherit that mental model and go deeper (capturing judgment, not just activity). Threat: Affinity has significant network effects and switching costs at larger firms. Reidar's beachhead in emerging funds (<$150M AUM) avoids direct competition.

**Timeline to mainstream:** Already standard among 1,700+ institutions using Affinity alone. Emerging fund adoption accelerating in 2025-2026.

**Source quality:** Tier 2 — Affinity product data; Tier 2 — Peony.ink VC software landscape report 2025

---

### Trend 3: Agentic AI — From Copilot to Autonomous Workflow

**Description:** The 2026 shift is from AI as assistant (answer a question) to AI as agent (do the work). By 2028, approximately 33% of enterprise software applications are expected to incorporate agentic AI (up from <1% in 2024), enabling automation of ~15% of daily work decisions. Bessemer Venture Partners reports reclaiming 234 hours per analyst via agentic tools. BlackRock achieved 5x research throughput (10-15 companies/day vs. 2-3 manually). In February 2026, Rowspace launched with $50M (Sequoia + Emergence Capital) specifically to build AI-native agentic data workflows for PE/VC firms.

**Current adoption stage:** Early (agentic layer), though foundational models mature

**Impact on Reidar:** Strong opportunity. Reidar's passive-learning architecture is exactly the agentic pattern the market is moving toward. The window to establish this before Affinity/PitchBook build it into their platforms is approximately 18-24 months. First-mover advantage in personalized, judgment-learning agents is meaningful.

**Timeline to mainstream:** 2027-2028 for broad enterprise adoption; 2026 for early-adopter VC firms

**Source quality:** Tier 1 — Gartner (33% agentic enterprise software by 2028); Tier 1 — Bessemer State of AI 2025 (234 hours saved); Tier 2 — Fortune (Rowspace $50M, Feb 2026)

---

### Trend 4: Commoditization of Shared Intelligence Platforms

**Description:** The data layer (PitchBook, Crunchbase, Harmonic) is commoditizing. Harmonic reached a $1.45B valuation in 2025 on the strength of early-stage signal detection (founder movements, hiring, domain registrations). But as these platforms scale, every firm sees the same signals. The competitive advantage of being an Harmonic subscriber shrinks as the subscriber base grows. This is the core dynamic Reidar's thesis exploits.

**Current adoption stage:** Mature (shared data platforms), which creates demand for differentiated intelligence

**Impact on Reidar:** Direct opportunity — Reidar's entire thesis depends on this commoditization accelerating. As Harmonic, PitchBook, and Crunchbase AI push toward universal adoption, the differentiation layer reverts to individual investor judgment. That is precisely what Reidar captures.

**Timeline to mainstream:** Already underway. Harmonic's $1.45B valuation signals peak market confidence in shared data — which is exactly when the next layer (personalized intelligence) becomes attractive.

**Source quality:** Tier 2 — Affinity/Qubit Capital industry reporting; PitchBook profile data

---

### Trend 5: Sector Specialization Replacing Generalist VC

**Description:** The share of generalist VC funds has dropped from 22% in 2020 to just 5% by early 2026. Sector specialization is now the dominant strategy for emerging funds. This matters for tooling: a sector-specialized fund needs tools that understand *their specific thesis* — not generic market intelligence. Between 2022-2025, the proportion of emerging fund managers without prior VC experience increased from 31% to 40%. These new managers are particularly hungry for tools that help them punch above their weight.

**Current adoption stage:** Growing (sector specialization) / Early (thesis-aware tooling)

**Impact on Reidar:** Strong opportunity. Reidar's thesis-learning model is the exact tool a sector-specialized emerging fund needs. Generic AI assistants don't know whether a fund cares about B2B SaaS in Southeast Asia or climate tech in the US Midwest. Reidar does, because it learns from each GP's behavior.

**Timeline to mainstream:** Sector specialization trend already here; thesis-aware tooling is a 2026-2027 market opening

**Source quality:** Tier 2 — VC Lab / Foundry Group industry data; Decile Group emerging manager analysis

## Behavioral Shifts

### Shift 1: GPs are moving from passive to active AI adoption — but still need workflow integration

**Data:** Between Q4 2023 and Q2 2025, usage of AI features in VC platforms nearly quadrupled. Funds that used AI tools more than ten times raised an average of $2.6M in soft commitments vs. $1.8M for non-users in their first 6-12 months. 85% of dealmakers automate daily tasks with AI (up from 76% year prior).

**Impact on Reidar:** GPs have already crossed the "will I use AI" threshold. The behavioral shift Reidar needs to exploit is the gap between "I use AI tools for research" (commodity) and "I have an AI that knows how I think" (defensible). GPs are primed for the next step — passive capture is no longer alien.

---

### Shift 2: Emerging fund managers are launching without traditional VC backgrounds — and need AI to compensate

**Data:** 40% of new emerging fund managers have no prior VC experience (up from 31% in 2022). The share of generalist funds dropped from 22% to 5% (2020-2026). A growing support system of AI-powered assistants is enabling operators, angels, and builders to launch credible funds.

**Impact on Reidar:** The beachhead market (1-5 GP emerging funds) is growing rapidly. These managers are not set in their ways — they are actively building their operational stack. They don't have a Kauffman Fellowship network or a 20-year Rolodex. They need tools that amplify judgment, not just data. Reidar is a natural fit.

---

### Shift 3: LP expectations are shifting toward data-driven portfolio reporting

**Data:** LPs now expect dynamic reporting built on structured data with metrics that refresh as the portfolio evolves. This is forcing GPs to instrument their workflows more rigorously.

**Impact on Reidar:** Indirect benefit. As GPs instrument their workflows for LP reporting, they generate more structured data — which creates more signal for Reidar's learning model to capture. The LP-driven instrumentation trend is a tailwind.

---

### Shift 4: AI is concentrating deal volume at the top — not expanding it

**Data:** AI captures 53% of global VC deal value in 2025, but deal volumes have actually declined. Capital is concentrating in mega-deals (15 companies raised $2B+ in 2025). Early-stage deal count is flat or down.

**Impact on Reidar:** Mixed signal. The macro environment creates urgency for emerging funds to find deals before capital concentration pushes valuations up. This increases the premium on proprietary sourcing and early conviction — exactly what Reidar enables. However, a tighter LP environment may compress emerging fund budgets for new software.

---

### Shift 5: The experimentation era is over — 2026 demands measurable outcomes

**Data:** The 2026 market consensus (GoHub Ventures, QED Investors, Sapphire Ventures, Foundation Capital) is that "experimentation phase is over." AI tools that cannot demonstrate ROI will be cut. Enterprises are consolidating AI vendors.

**Impact on Reidar:** This is a Go-to-Market constraint, not a product constraint. Reidar must be able to demonstrate a clear before/after: "deals you found before others did," "time saved on deal evaluation," "decision quality metrics." ROI storytelling is required for sale, not optional.

## Expert Predictions

### Prediction 1: "The AI-native VC firm will be the standard by 2028"
**Source:** GoHub Ventures, 2026 VC Investment Trends report (Tier 2)
**Prediction:** If 2025 was about laying AI infrastructure, 2026 is when the application layer must prove measurable outcomes. By 2028, AI-native workflows will be the default expectation for any new fund launch.
**Credibility:** Moderate. GoHub is a practitioner perspective, not analyst research, but directionally consistent with Gartner agentic AI data (33% of enterprise apps agentic by 2028).
**Reidar implication:** 18-24 month window to establish category leadership before the market matures.

---

### Prediction 2: "Budget will consolidate around fewer, deeper AI vendors"
**Source:** TechCrunch (Dec 2025), multiple VC survey data (Tier 2)
**Prediction:** Enterprises will spend more on AI in 2026 through fewer contracts. Consolidation pressure means tools that don't become "must-have" will be cut.
**Credibility:** High. Supported by multiple independent surveys and operator commentary.
**Reidar implication:** Reidar needs to be positioned as a core workflow tool, not a point solution. The pitch is "AI investment associate" (replaces/augments a team member), not "AI search tool" (replaceable feature).

---

### Prediction 3: "Conviction capital will define the next VC era"
**Source:** Venionaire Capital, "North America VC 2026: AI & Conviction Capital" (Tier 2)
**Prediction:** The next VC era belongs to funds with proprietary thesis and conviction — not those with access to the same data everyone else has. "Conviction capital" means backing companies earlier, with higher confidence, based on sharper pattern recognition.
**Credibility:** Moderate (single source), but consistent with broader "shared data = commoditized alpha" narrative.
**Reidar implication:** This is the exact value proposition Reidar enables. The prediction validates the core thesis.

---

### Prediction 4: "AI tools for VC operations will evolve from data aggregation to judgment amplification"
**Source:** HBR, "How Generative AI Is Reshaping Venture Capital" (Nov 2025) (Tier 1)
**Prediction:** The current generation of VC AI tools primarily aggregates and screens data. The next generation will amplify individual investor judgment — the transition from "what does the market know" to "what do I know that the market doesn't."
**Credibility:** High. HBR is Tier 1. The framing aligns with Bessemer, Foundation Capital, and QED predictions independently.
**Reidar implication:** This prediction is the product roadmap. HBR is describing Reidar before Reidar exists.

---

### Prediction 5: "Smaller funds will outperform on AI adoption speed"
**Source:** VC Lab / Decile Group platform data (Tier 2)
**Prediction:** Emerging fund managers are adopting AI tools faster than established funds, partly because they have no legacy systems to displace. AI adoption gap between emerging and established funds is narrowing rapidly.
**Credibility:** Moderate (self-serving data from a platform that serves emerging funds), but directionally credible.
**Reidar implication:** Beachhead market is not just strategically accessible — it is actively hungry for exactly this class of tool.

## Timing Assessment

### Is now a good time to enter this market?

**Yes — with specific caveats.**

**The case for entering now (March 2026):**

1. **Adoption is proven, category is not locked.** 82-95% of VC firms use AI in some form. The behavioral barrier is gone. But no single tool owns "personalized investor intelligence." The category does not yet have a clear leader.

2. **The commoditization moment is here.** Harmonic's $1.45B valuation signals that shared signal platforms have peaked their differentiation value. The timing for a "personal judgment layer" is exactly right when shared data tools are at maximum penetration.

3. **Emerging fund market is growing and underserved.** 40% of new fund managers have no VC background. The share of generalist funds has collapsed (22% → 5%). These managers need thesis-aware, judgment-learning tools — not generic databases.

4. **Rowspace's $50M Sequoia round validates the "AI-native VC operations" category** but targets large PE/VC ($100B+ AUM). The sub-$150M AUM emerging fund market has no comparable bet yet.

5. **HBR (Tier 1) is already writing the thesis.** When institutional publications describe your exact product as "the next phase of VC AI" without knowing you exist, that is a timing signal.

**The case for caution:**

1. **Affinity, Harmonic, and PitchBook have large engineering teams.** If they decide to build a judgment-learning layer, they have distribution and data advantages. The 18-24 month window is real but not unlimited.

2. **The 2026 "prove ROI" pressure means sales cycles will be longer.** GPs are consolidating tools, not adding them. Reidar must win on displacement of existing spend or clear additive value.

3. **Emerging funds have constrained budgets.** A fund with $50M AUM may not pay enterprise SaaS prices. Pricing strategy must account for the beachhead market's capacity.

---

**What would make the timing better:**
- An Affinity or PitchBook acquisition signals they are building up-market, further validating the emerging fund gap
- A high-profile emerging fund publicly attributing a deal win to AI-driven sourcing (creates category awareness)
- LPs starting to ask GPs about their AI sourcing stack (creates pull from above)

**What would make the timing worse:**
- Affinity ships a "learn my investment style" feature in a major product update
- Harmonic pivots toward personalization at scale
- A broader VC market downturn reduces emerging fund launches and software budgets

---

### Specific Implications for Reidar's Market Entry Strategy

1. **Lead with the judgment thesis, not the AI thesis.** The market is saturated with "AI for VC" pitches. Reidar's differentiation is *personal judgment capture*, not AI generally. "The only tool that learns how you think" is the frame.

2. **Target the 2022-2025 vintage emerging fund cohort.** These funds are 1-3 years in, have an established thesis, and have enough deal history to train a model. They are not yet locked into Affinity at enterprise scale.

3. **Use Granola as a wedge story.** Granola raised $43M at $250M in 2025 and VCs are its most vocal champions — proving that VCs will pay for passive capture tools that integrate into their meeting workflow. Reidar is the judgment layer that sits on top.

4. **Price below Affinity Enterprise to win the emerging fund budget.** Affinity is $125-200/user/month. Reidar should target $50-100/user/month for the beachhead, with a clear upgrade path as AUM grows.

5. **Build toward LP reporting as a second-order value prop.** LPs are demanding structured, data-driven reporting. Reidar can generate provenance data on investment decisions (why this company, what signals triggered action) that becomes a competitive LP relations tool.

6. **The 18-24 month window is not guaranteed.** Affinity knows their CRM data contains implicit judgment signals. The speed-to-product advantage matters more than perfection.

2. **2024-2025 M&A activity in VC tooling specifically** — No major acquisitions were confirmed in this research period. It is unclear whether this reflects an actual absence of M&A or a data gap (e.g., acqui-hires or undisclosed deals).

3. **Affinity's current valuation** — Affinity's Series C ($80M, led by Menlo Ventures) is documented but no subsequent valuation update was found. Their most recent headcount (321 employees as of Feb 2026) suggests continued growth.

4. **Harmonic's exact revenue and funding details** — The $1.45B valuation figure appeared in multiple sources but the funding round details (round size, lead investor, date) were not confirmed.

5. **Pricing data for emerging fund tools** — Limited public pricing data for tools targeting sub-$150M AUM funds. Most pricing pages gate behind sales conversations.

6. **GP survey data specifically on "judgment/thesis learning" tools** — No survey specifically asked GPs whether they want tools that learn their individual investment style. This is a market research gap Reidar should fill with primary research.

7. **Churn rates and NPS data for incumbent VC CRM tools** — Would help quantify the switching opportunity. Unavailable from public sources.
