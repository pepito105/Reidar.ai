## The One Question These Projections Answer

> **How much should Reidar raise, what does it buy, and what does the company look like at the end of that runway?**

Short answer: **Raise $400K.** It buys 12+ months of runway at founder stipend level, covers all product and GTM costs, and positions the company to raise a seed round from a defensible traction position.

## Raise Sizing Logic

### 12-Month Burn (The Target Period)

Both founders go full-time from Day 1 of the raise. Co-founder quits their current job when the round closes.

| Period | Monthly Burn | Duration | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Months 1–12 (both founders full-time) | $8,000–8,645 | 12 months | $96,000–103,740 |
| One-time costs (legal, events, launch) | — | One-time | $10,000–18,000 |
| **Total 12-Month Burn** | | | **$106,000–121,740** |

### Raise Recommendation: **$400K**

| Use of Funds | Amount | % |
|---|---|---|
| Founder compensation (12 months) | $84,000 | 21% |
| Infrastructure + tools (12 months) | $9,000 | 2.25% |
| GTM: events, content, outreach | $15,000 | 3.75% |
| Legal: incorporation, SAFEs, IP | $8,000 | 2% |
| One-time product/design costs | $4,000 | 1% |
| Buffer (unexpected costs, ~30%) | $30,000 | 7.5% |
| **Operating total** | **$150,000** | **37.5%** |
| **Seed round war chest** | **$250,000** | **62.5%** |

The $250,000 war chest is intentional. At $400K raised with ~$150K in operating burn over 12 months, Reidar ends the pre-seed period with ~$250K in cash. This isn't left on the table — it's the bridge:

- It signals to the next investor that the founders have been disciplined with capital
- It extends runway to 18–20 months if the seed round takes longer than expected
- It means Reidar is raising the seed from a position of strength, not desperation

**Why not $250K?** A $250K raise barely covers 12 months of operations and leaves no buffer. It's the kind of raise that requires everything to go exactly right. Not recommended.

**Why not $750K?** At $750K, the dilution cost may not be justified by the spend. Reidar can prove the core hypothesis (design partners convert, retention holds) on $400K. A larger pre-seed often leads to premature scaling — hiring before PMF, over-investing in marketing before the product loop is validated.

## Three Scenarios

### Conservative Scenario

**Core assumption:** Design partner conversion is slow. 2 of 5 convert to paid at Month 4. Word of mouth is minimal. Growth is 1–2 net adds/month in the back half.

| Metric | Month 6 | Month 12 | Month 18 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paying customers | 3 | 8 | 14 |
| MRR | $1,500 | $4,100 | $7,300 |
| ARR run-rate | $18,000 | $49,200 | $87,600 |
| Cash remaining (of $400K) | ~$314K | ~$219K | ~$110K |

**Seed raise readiness at Month 12:** Possible but difficult. 8 customers and $49K ARR is a thin story. The key question: is churn low? If retention is strong (< 3%/month) and NPS is high, the data quality argument compensates for the small top-line number. Raise the seed at Month 18 instead.

**What to do in this scenario:**
- Double down on design partner program — 5 more free accounts, target GPs with larger public followings for referral amplification
- Delay any paid marketing or conference spend until Month 10+
- Use Insight Partners contact more aggressively for warm intro to emerging fund network

## Milestones That Unlock the Seed Round

These are the specific proof points that transform the pre-seed narrative into a seed narrative. Each milestone is binary — either achieved or not.

| Milestone | Target Date | Why It Matters to a Seed Investor |
|---|---|---|
| 5 paying customers | Month 5–6 | Proves WTP; de-risks "will anyone pay for this" |
| First Growth tier customer | Month 7–9 | Proves the upgrade path exists; ARPU expansion is real |
| 3 publicly referenceable customers | Month 8 | Enables cold outreach with social proof; GPs talk to each other |
| Sub-3% monthly churn for 90 days | Month 9–12 | Validates the moat thesis; proves history creates lock-in |
| One inbound customer (not outbound) | Month 9–12 | Proves word of mouth is starting; the flywheel has begun |
| $7,500+ MRR | Month 12 | $90K ARR = minimum credible seed story in today's market |

**The seed story in one paragraph:**

> "Reidar launched to NYC emerging fund managers 12 months ago. We have 15 paying customers at $500–$1,000/month, $7,800 MRR, and sub-3% monthly churn — GPs who've built 6+ months of decision history don't want to restart. We've proven the model at the beachhead. We're raising $2M to hire our first GTM hire, expand to Boston and Miami, and run the same design partner motion that got us to 15 in NYC."

## Investor Framing: What This Raise Is Really For

The $400K raise is not for building the product. The product is built — Reidar has a working React + FastAPI prototype with all core features functional today. The raise is for:

1. **Runway to prove the hypothesis** — 12 months to learn whether passive capture surfaces real signal, and whether GPs will pay monthly for it
2. **GTM cost** — Design partner program, 2–3 NYC events, content flywheel
3. **Co-founder transition** — Enables the technical co-founder to quit and go full-time without financial risk
4. **Credibility** — A funded company with a named investor gets calls returned; an unfunded founder with a prototype does not

This is the honest version of the pitch. Investors at pre-seed are not buying revenue — they're buying the right to back the founder's ability to learn fast and find PMF.

## Pre-Seed Round Structure

**Recommended instrument:** SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) with a valuation cap

| Parameter | Recommendation | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Instrument | SAFE (YC Standard) | Industry standard; no negotiation friction; fast close |
| Raise amount | $400,000 | 12 months of runway + buffer |
| Valuation cap | $3M–$4M post-money | Comparable pre-seed caps for B2B AI SaaS with working prototype in 2026; reflects that product exists but has no revenue yet |
| Discount | 20% optional | Offer to earliest converts (first $100K) as incentive for speed |
| Pro-rata rights | For checks ≥ $50K | Gives lead investors ability to maintain ownership at seed; useful for closing larger checks |

**Dilution math at $3.5M cap:**
- $400K / $3.5M = 11.4% dilution
- Founders retain 88.6% pre-seed
- After seed round at $8–10M post-money valuation (typical for $90K+ ARR B2B SaaS), founders retain ~65–70% combined

## Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design partners don't convert to paid | Medium | High | Fix the product before spending on GTM; extend free period rather than force conversion |
| Churn higher than 5%/month | Low-Medium | High | Watch the Month 1–3 cohort closely; if 2+ customers churn before Month 6, the daily value loop is broken |
| Raise takes longer than 3 weeks | High | Low | Cash buffer absorbs delay; 3-week timeline is aggressive — plan for 6–8 weeks to close at pre-seed |
| Affinity or Notion announces competitive feature | Low | Medium | Moat is accumulated history; an announcement doesn't replicate 6 months of GP data |

**On the 3-week close timeline:** Highly ambitious. Pre-seed rounds on SAFEs can close in 3 weeks if the investor set is warm and small (3–5 angels or a single micro-VC writing $200–400K). Plan for 6–8 weeks to avoid running out of goodwill chasing a close. The fundraising sprint framework (Week 1 prep, Week 2 active, Week 3 close) is the right structure — just build a buffer into the plan.
