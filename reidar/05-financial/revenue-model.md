## Pricing Strategy

### Confirmed Tiers

| Tier | Price | Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seed** | $500/month ($6,000/year) | 1–2 GP emerging funds, pre-Series A deal flow | Below "do I need to ask permission?" threshold for most GPs. Impulse-range for a tool that demonstrably saves 2+ hours/week. |
| **Growth** | $1,000/month ($12,000/year) | 2–5 GP funds, $50M–$150M AUM, multi-user | Per-firm pricing — not per seat — at this tier; covers the full team without seat-count friction. |

**Pricing rationale against the competitive landscape:**
- Affinity: $2,000–$3,000/month — priced for Series A+ funds with ops budgets
- 4Degrees: $1,500+/month — similar enterprise positioning
- Generic CRMs (Attio, HubSpot): $50–$200/month — feature-light, not purpose-built
- Reidar's sweet spot: purpose-built intelligence at 1/4 the cost of Affinity, 10x the value vs. generic CRMs

**Why not lower?** Research found GPs are highly skeptical of cheap VC tools — low pricing signals low seriousness. $500/month positions Reidar as a real product, not a side project. It also establishes willingness-to-pay baseline before growth-tier upselling.

**Why not higher?** Emerging fund GPs at Fund I–II have informal budget processes. Decisions above $500–$1,000/month require LP or IC discussion at most small funds. Staying at or below $1,000/month keeps Reidar in the "GP can swipe their card" category.

### Pricing Assumptions

- Seed/Growth mix assumed at 80/20 in Year 1 (most early adopters are smaller funds)
- Mix shifts to 60/40 in Year 2 as word of mouth reaches slightly larger funds
- No annual discount offered in Year 1 — preserve MRR clarity for investors
- Design partners (free for 90 days) not counted in revenue; counted as pilot cohort

## Sensitivity Analysis

The primary revenue variable is **customer acquisition rate**. Everything else (churn, ARPU) is downstream.

### What if acquisition is slower? (Bear Case)

- 10 paying customers at Month 12 instead of 15
- Year 1 ARR: ~$62,400 run-rate
- Time to break-even extends from Month 16 to Month 20+
- **Mitigation:** Extend design partner cohort; prioritize Insight Partners referral chain

### What if acquisition is faster? (Bull Case)

- 20 paying customers at Month 12
- Year 1 ARR: ~$124,800 run-rate
- Raises leverage for Series A conversation at Month 18

### What if the Seed tier converts to Growth faster? (Upside)

- If 30% of customers are on Growth tier by Month 12 (vs. 15% base case):
- Year 1 ARR: ~$108,000 run-rate
- No change to CAC or headcount — pure ARPU expansion

### Key Breaking Assumption

> **The model breaks if fewer than 3 design partners convert to paid at Month 4.**

If 0 of 5 design partners pay after the free period, it likely means:
1. The daily value (pre-meeting briefs) didn't land
2. The passive capture feature isn't surfacing meaningful signal yet
3. Price is genuinely too high for this cohort

This is the single most important validation signal in Month 4. If it fires red, pause paid acquisition and fix the product before spending more on GTM.

## Unit Economics

| Metric | Year 1 (Month 12) | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| ARPU | $520/month | $620/month | $750/month |
| ACV | $6,240 | $7,440 | $9,000 |
| Gross Margin | ~94% | ~93% | ~92% |
| Estimated CAC | $1,500–3,000 | $1,000–2,000 | $800–1,500 |
| LTV (3-yr model, 3% churn) | $17,300 | $20,667 | $25,000 |
| LTV:CAC | 5.8–11.5x | 10–20x | 16–31x |
| Payback period | 3–6 months | 2–3 months | 1–2 months |

**Note on CAC:** Year 1 CAC is high because founder time is the primary acquisition channel (design partner program, content, conference attendance). By Year 2, referral and content flywheel reduce paid CAC substantially. These are not cash CAC — they include founder time valued at market rate (~$75/hour for comparability).

## Revenue Model for the Pitch

When presenting to investors, frame the revenue model as three concentric circles:

1. **Beachhead economics (now):** 10–15 emerging GPs in NYC at $500/month = $60–90K ARR. Small but meaningful. Real willingness to pay. Referral-dense market.

2. **Core market expansion (Year 2):** 40–50 customers at $600 blended ARPU = $288–360K ARR. This is where the product's compounding moat starts to be visible in retention data.

3. **Upmarket motion (Year 3+):** A handful of $1,500–$2,000/month enterprise accounts for mid-size funds — the Affinity and 4Degrees replacement motion. Each enterprise account = ~3 beachhead accounts in revenue.

The pitch isn't "we have $7,800 MRR at Month 12." The pitch is: **"We proved the model at the emerging GP beachhead, retention is strong, and the same motion scales to 2,500+ US VC firms."**
