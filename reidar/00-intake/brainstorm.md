## Variation 1 — Personal Intelligence Layer (current bet)

**Concept:** Reidar as positioned — passive context capture, personal investor profile, decision memory, compounding moat. Like Granola for your entire VC workflow.

**What's exciting:**
- Genuinely differentiated thesis — no one is doing this well
- Compounding moat: the longer you use it, the more valuable it becomes and the harder it is to leave
- Addresses a real systemic problem (shared data commoditization) not just a workflow annoyance

**What's risky:**
- Hardest to build — passive capture requires deep integrations across calendar, email, meetings, CRM
- "Captures your judgment" is hard to prove until it's been running for 30–90 days
- Long time-to-value makes early retention difficult
- Regulatory/privacy concerns around capturing work communications

**How it changes competitive landscape:** No direct competitor. Most adjacent is Affinity (relationship intelligence) but they don't capture judgment.

---

## Variation 2 — Deal Memory Product

**Concept:** Narrow to one job: never lose a deal you've already seen. Every company evaluated, why you passed, what would change your mind — captured and resurfaced when conditions change. No sourcing, no memos, no pipeline. Just memory.

**What's exciting:**
- Immediate, visceral value proposition — every VC has a war story about missing a company they saw early
- Laser-focused — easy to explain, easy to demo, fast to build a v1
- Could be the wedge into the broader personal intelligence layer
- "Reidar remembered so you didn't have to" is a powerful moment

**What's risky:**
- Narrow surface area — limited revenue ceiling as a standalone product
- Easier for Affinity or a CRM to add as a feature
- Doesn't address the sourcing/finding problem, only the memory problem

**How it changes competitive landscape:** More defensible as a wedge into the full platform than a standalone business.

---

## Variation 3 — Analyst Layer at Large Funds

**Concept:** Target junior analysts and associates at mid-to-large funds (not emerging GPs). These are the people drowning in deal flow who need to surface the best companies for their GPs. Sell to the fund, deploy for the analysts.

**What's exciting:**
- Larger ACV — established funds have real tooling budgets ($10K–$100K/yr)
- Warm entry point through Insight Partners contact
- Analysts are the power users; GPs approve the check
- High-quality early customers for fundraising story

**What's risky:**
- Longer sales cycles (procurement, security review, legal)
- Need GP champion — harder to get started without top-down buy-in
- Misaligned with emerging GP beachhead — building for Insight ≠ building for a $30M fund

**How it changes competitive landscape:** Puts Reidar more directly against PitchBook and Harmonic. Need clearer differentiation.

---

## Variation 4 — Mandate-as-a-Service Entry Point

**Concept:** Lead with helping GPs define and articulate their investment thesis precisely. "Write your mandate, get your personalized deal feed." The investor profile and passive capture come later as the product deepens.

**What's exciting:**
- Much lower barrier to first value — immediate output on day one
- Mandate definition is genuinely painful (most GPs struggle to articulate precisely)
- Creates the foundation for everything else in the product
- Easy onboarding story

**What's risky:**
- Feels like a feature, not a product
- Low defensibility — could be a ChatGPT prompt
- Doesn't capture the compounding intelligence story

**How it changes competitive landscape:** Less differentiated. Easier to copy.

---

## Variation 5 — LP Intelligence Layer

**Concept:** Flip the customer: sell to LPs (family offices, endowments, fund-of-funds) who invest in dozens of VC funds. Same problem from the other side: evaluating fund managers, tracking portfolio exposure, surfacing signal.

**What's exciting:**
- Much larger wallet — LPs have massive AUM and real tooling budgets
- Underserved by current tooling — almost nothing built specifically for LP intelligence
- Clear ROI: better fund selection = better returns on $100M+ allocations

**What's risky:**
- Completely different product and buyer — requires deep LP network access
- Founders have no LP relationships
- Longer sales cycle, more institutional
- Changes the entire product design

**How it changes competitive landscape:** Largely uncontested space. But very different company to build.

---

## Variation 6 — Context Layer Infrastructure / API

**Concept:** Don't build the frontend. Build the context/memory API that other VC tools plug into. Reidar becomes the shared intelligence layer underneath every tool in the stack.

**What's exciting:**
- Platform moat — every tool becomes a distribution channel
- Developer-friendly go-to-market
- Long-term defensibility if the ecosystem grows around you

**What's risky:**
- Very hard to monetize early — no direct end-user value
- Requires ecosystem traction to matter
- Founders with no developer community or VC tool relationships

**How it changes competitive landscape:** No direct competitor but very long path to revenue.

---

## Synthesis & Recommendation

**Strongest long-term bet:** Variation 1 (Personal Intelligence Layer) — the thesis is right and the moat is real if the product works.

**Fastest path to proof:** Variation 2 (Deal Memory) as a wedge. One job, immediate value, proves the core hypothesis before building the full platform.

**Go-to-market insight from Variation 3:** The Insight Partners contact is a gift — use it to get one large fund as a reference customer even if the eventual beachhead is emerging GPs. A "Insight Partners uses Reidar" case study is worth more than 20 emerging GP pilots for fundraising.

**Recommended framing to present to investors:** Lead with Variation 1 (the thesis), prove it with Variation 2 (the wedge), use Variation 3 (the large fund reference) as the credibility signal.
